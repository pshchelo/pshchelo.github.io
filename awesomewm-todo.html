Let’s be awesome

Date Tue 26 January 2016 Tags awesomewm

Recently I got fascinated with Awesome WM. It is a minimalistic tiling window manager for Linux et al, even defaults look nice as to my taste, is quite keyboard-centric and promises to be easily extensible.

After using it for several days in my usual computing ways both for work and play, I decided to try and make a run for it as my default desktop. Hands-on opportunity to learn another scripting language (Awesome is configured and extended in Lua) seems also interesting.

During these days I also identified some parts/functionality that I am missing “out-of-the-box” in comparison to my previous DE (for last several years I was using exclusively Ubuntu with Unity as desktop). Here I will try to document what I am changing/implementing as I go. The list below should slowly change from text to links to either next posts, or lines of code/commits in my dotfiles GitHub repo, as I will put all my awesome-related configs there too.

Note
Both my usual systems, notebook and home desktop, are shared with less Linux-savvy family members (just finally hooked them off Window$), so for now I am going to try and re-use already installed Gnome/Unity components where possible, as I am not going to completely remove those from my hard drives. In the future, I do might try to switch to very minimalistic install, so I keep it in mind when choosing options. But not for now :)
